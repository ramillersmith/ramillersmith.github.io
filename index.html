Transportation

We highly encourage walking and taking transit to get around San Francisco! If you have any questions about how to get around while you're here, please let us know.

General Information
BART operates regional trains between San Francisco and other Bay Area destinations, including both the San Francisco and Oakland airports. Muni operates most other transit services within the city. Subway stations on Market Street in downtown San Francisco are served by both BART and Muni via separate belowground platforms.

Transportation Costs
Buy a physical Clipper Card from ticket machines at transit stations for $3, or download on your phone via the Clipper app.
  • BART from SFO to downtown San Francisco: $9.65
  • BART from Oakland Airport to downtown San Francisco: $11
  • Muni Light Rail (), Streetcars (), and Buses: $2.50
  • Cable Cars: $8 per ride, or $13 for day pass on MuniMobile

    Things To Do

    Here are a few of our favorite (mostly free) museums and parks to check out while you're here!

    The Embarcadero
    Walk along the city's main waterfront from Fisherman's Wharf to Oracle Park and enjoy views of the Bay!

    Musée Mécanique
    Collection of cool old penny arcade games (admission is free, but games require quarters). Getting there: Take the streetcar to Jefferson & Taylor in Fisherman's Wharf.

    Cable Car Museum
    Free museum, with a view of the giant machinery that powers the cable car system! Getting there: Take a Powell Cable Car and ask the conductor to let you off at the Cable Car Museum.

    Ina Coolbrith Park
    Hilltop park with great views of downtown and the Bay. Getting there: Take a Powell Cable Car and ask the conductor to let you off at Vallejo Street. Then, just walk uphill on Vallejo!

    Aquatic Park
    Waterfront park with a great view of the Golden Gate Bridge. Getting there: Take the streetcar or the Powell-Hyde Cable Car to the end of the line in Fisherman's Wharf.

    Dolores Park
    Urban park with a large field and great view of downtown. Getting there: Take the westbound to 20th Street.

    Ocean Beach and the Great Highway
    Sandy beach and pedestrian walkway along the city's western coast. Getting there: Take the westbound to the end of the line at Ocean Beach.
